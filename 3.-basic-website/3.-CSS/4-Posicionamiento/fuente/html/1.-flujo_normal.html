Normal flow

How web browser places boxes by default

Its placement and behave depends on the type of element

There are two types of elements: block and inline

I am a basic block level element. My adjacent block level elements sit on new lines below me.

Block elements ocuppies the whole line altough they don't need it

By default we span 100% of the width of our parent element, and we are as tall as our child content. Our total width and height is our content + padding + border width/height (content-box).

We are separated by our margins. Because of margin collapsing, we are separated by the width of one of our margins, not both.

Inline elements don't alter flow content and place at the same line than the rest of elements, if there is room enough. A specific width or height can't be set for them. Overflowing inline elements will wrap onto a new line if possible (like this one containing text is doing)

Inline elements can't modify their width or height, they occupy the space enough to accommodate their contentcan modify its margin or padding, but doing sodon't alter line weight
